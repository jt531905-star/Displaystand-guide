How to Assemble a Yoroi-dai
Collapsible Armor Stand Assembly Guide

To display a suit of Japanese armor (yoroi), you need a yoroi-dai — an armor stand. There are two main types: a fixed stand that is crafted to stand upright from the start, and a collapsible stand whose parts come apart so it can be neatly stored inside an armor chest (yoroi-bitsu) for easy transport.

This guide walks you through assembling the collapsible type step by step.

1

Check All the Parts

Before you begin, lay out all the parts of the collapsible armor stand. There are five pieces in total: the central body (torso), two legs, one shoulder piece, and one neck piece.

2

Attach the Legs to the Body

Start by fitting both legs into the central body piece. Each leg has a matching groove, so simply align the grooves and press them together — they should slot in snugly.

3

Insert the Shoulder Piece

Next, slide the shoulder piece down through the hole at the top of the body. It threads through from above and locks into position.

4

Insert the Neck Piece (Helmet Rest)

Finally, insert the neck piece — the part that will hold the helmet (kabuto). When viewed from above, it should form a cross (+) shape as it locks into the top of the shoulder piece.

✓

Assembly Complete!

⚔️

Your Yoroi-dai is Ready

Assembly is now complete. Place the stand on top of the armor chest (yoroi-bitsu) and proudly display your samurai armor.
